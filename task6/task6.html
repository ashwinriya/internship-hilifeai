Beans

Green beans are a common staple in households across the country. They go by several names, some of the most popular ones being snap beans and string beans.

Green beans are native to North, South, and Central America. Today, however, they grow all over the world. They grow year-round, meaning you find them in grocery stores no matter what season it is.

Cabbage

Cabbage, comprising several cultivars of Brassica oleracea, is a leafy green, red (purple), or white (pale green) biennial plant grown as an annual vegetable crop for its dense-leaved heads.

A cabbage generally weighs between 500 to 1,000 grams (1 to 2 lb). Smooth-leafed, firm-headed green cabbages are the most common, with smooth-leafed purple cabbages and crinkle-leafed savoy cabbages of both colours being rarer.
